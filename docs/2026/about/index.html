About

Tech Money is a personal project to make tech-industry political giving easier to read.

What's on this site

Every number here is built from Federal Election Commission filings — the disclosures that campaigns, PACs, and party committees submit by law. The focus is contributions where the donor's listed employer matches a tracked tech company (Google, Meta, Nvidia, Anthropic, and so on), plus giving from those companies' own PACs.

Why it's not trivial

The FEC publishes raw filings. Employers on those filings are free text — "Google," "Google LLC," "google inc," "goog," and "alphabet" all come in as different strings. Identifying tech donors means building and maintaining a lookup that maps messy employer strings to clean company names, one by one. That manual tagging is the core of what this site does; it is also the main limit.

What's missing

  • Employer matching is incomplete. Someone who wrote "Self-employed" or an unusual abbreviation of a tech employer won't be counted here.
  • The tracked-company list is curated, not exhaustive.
  • Unitemized contributions (under $200 per cycle) aren't reported by name to the FEC at all, so they can't be here either.
