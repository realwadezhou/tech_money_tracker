Methodology

How the numbers on this site are built: what's counted, what isn't, and what each column label means. New to campaign finance terms? Start with Campaign Finance 101.

Where the data comes from

Everything on this site comes from Federal Election Commission bulk files: individual contributions, candidate master records, committee master records, candidate-committee links, and independent expenditures. The FEC publishes these as raw CSVs; this site groups and aggregates them.

How donors are matched to tech companies

When a donor gives more than $200 in a cycle, the filing committee must record the donor's name, address, and employer. The employer is free text, so "Google," "Google LLC," "google inc," "goog," and "alphabet" all appear as different strings in the raw data. Matching tech donors means keeping a lookup that maps these messy strings to a clean canonical company name, built and maintained by hand. Contributions get counted toward a company only when the employer string matches an entry in that lookup.

What is counted

  • Itemized individual contributions to federal committees, minus refunds of those contributions.
  • Contributions are attributed to a tech company when the donor's employer string maps to a tracked company in the hand-built lookup.
  • Candidate-level totals use the FEC's candidate-to-committee links to roll receipts up from each of a candidate's affiliated committees.
  • Independent expenditures (IE Support / IE Oppose) come from the FEC's independent-expenditure file and are reported separately from direct receipts.
  • Party lean for non-party committees (PACs, Super PACs) is inferred from their candidate-facing spending when the committee itself has no direct party affiliation.

What isn't counted

  • Unitemized contributions (under $200 per cycle) — the FEC does not publish these by name.
  • Donors whose employer string doesn't match a tracked tech company — including "Self-employed," odd abbreviations, blanks, and typos that haven't been mapped yet.
  • Companies not on the curated tracked-company list.
  • Outbound spending by committees (ads, operations, transfers) beyond the IE views mentioned above.

Current limits

  • Cycles currently covered: 2024 and 2026.
  • A committee's "Tech Share" is a share of itemized individual receipts only — not a share of all money received.
  • Weekly charts begin their display at 2023-01-01; totals shown include earlier rows present in the source files.
  • Per-candidate detail pages beyond national, state, Senate, and House-district views aren't built yet.

Column glossary

Plain-language meanings for every column label currently used on the site.

SectionThe site area or page family listed in a navigation table.
What It ShowsA short plain-language description of that page or export.
CompanyThe tracked tech company or firm tied to a donor-employer tag.
Candidate CommitteeA candidate-linked committee receiving contributions.
CommitteeA political committee, which may be a candidate committee, PAC, party committee, Super PAC, or another filer.
DonorA contributor name as grouped in the current dataset.
NameThe donor name shown on the major-donor table.
StateThe state-level Senate contest bucket or state navigation row.
DistrictA U.S. House district row.
JurisdictionA non-state jurisdiction such as DC or a territory.
CandidateAn individual candidate from the FEC candidate master records and candidate-committee link files.
D CandidateThe Democratic candidate with the most tech receipts in that Senate or House summary row.
R CandidateThe Republican candidate with the most tech receipts in that Senate or House summary row.
PartyThe candidate or committee party label shown in the export.
LeanA simplified D, R, Mixed, or Unknown label based on where classified tech money flowed, not a race rating.
TypeThe committee type from FEC filings, such as candidate committee, PAC, or party committee.
BucketA broad committee category used on this site, such as candidate, party, leadership PAC, or outside group.
SectorThe company-sector label from the tracked-company lookup.
TotalThe total dollars for that row's main entity in that specific table. On company, donor, and committee tables, this is the full amount shown for that entity.
Total ($)The donor's full total in dollars.
Total ReceiptsAll itemized individual contributions flowing into a candidate's affiliated committees, not just the tech-matched subset and not outside spending.
Tech ReceiptsThe portion of candidate or committee receipts that came from donors whose employer matches a tracked tech company.
Tech-Linked GivingThe site-wide sum of tech-matched contribution dollars in the current dataset.
Tech ShareThe share of itemized individual receipts coming from tracked tech donors.
Tech DonorsThe count of distinct donors in the tech-matched subset for that row.
DonorsThe count of distinct donors represented by that row.
CommitteesThe number of committees represented or linked in that table row.
Linked CommitteesThe number of committees affiliated with a candidate, from the FEC candidate-committee link file.
Committee NamesThe names of committees linked to that candidate.
Top CommitteeThe committee receiving the largest share of money from that donor or company row.
Top Committee FundedThe committee that received the most money from that donor in the public export.
Company TagsThe tracked tech-company labels attached to a donor or committee through matched contributions.
Pct ClassifiedThe share of dollars that could be assigned a party direction under the site's classification rules.
Pct Dem by DonorThe share of classified donors, not dollars, whose giving leaned Democratic in that company summary.
$ to DemDollars from that donor row that the pipeline classified as Democratic-leaning.
% to DemThe Democratic share of that donor's classified giving.
$ to RepDollars from that donor row that the pipeline classified as Republican-leaning.
% to RepThe Republican share of that donor's classified giving.
IE SupportIndependent expenditures by outside committees reported as supporting the candidate. These are not direct receipts to the candidate committee.
IE OpposeIndependent expenditures by outside committees reported as opposing the candidate. These are not direct receipts to the candidate committee.
RowsThe number of individual contribution rows rolled into that summary entry.
House DistrictsThe number of House districts represented in that state or jurisdiction row.
Senate CandidatesThe number of Senate candidates represented in that state or jurisdiction row.
FileA downloadable export file.
JSONA JSON payload produced for the site.
CycleThe election cycle this page covers.
Data As OfThe latest transaction date present in the current dataset.
Tracked CompaniesThe number of companies and firms currently in the hand-built employer lookup.

Why the site looks like this

The design goal is plain HTML, minimal CSS, a warm document background, and JavaScript only where charts or table sorting and filtering require it.
